Kalman Filter and Markov Localization ....Rudimentary implementation of the basics of a autonomous car

A major challenge in making a self driving car , is actually knowing where the car is located with an accuracy of 2-10 cm . GPS only gives us an accuracy of 5-10 m. So we use probabilistic techniques to model the car and its surroundings . On measurement we increase our certainty of our location and on movement , we reduce the certainty . This is just the basics , if we want to do something like the Sebastin Thurn 's Self Driving Car at Google , then we have a long way to go !

Face tracking Robot...Minimalistic

A robot capable of tracking a face . It isn't a robot yet , but I plan to use this system as the head of Hachiko.
The system works by using the Harr Cascade Filter avalible in most Computer Vision Libraries. This sub routine will gives me the coordinates of the face . Then I define vectors to find the center of the face , as well as that of the iPhone screen . Then I compute the difference between these vectors and I then use it as an input for Proportinal control system that I have running on the arduino . Based on the error , or difference between the vectors , I make compensations to minimize the error. Then ,I send data from the iPhone to arduino via bluetooth. The reason I use an iPhone is because I do not have a camera that works with the ardunio/raspberry pi ! .
