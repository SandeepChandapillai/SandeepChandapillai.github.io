Self Made Robotic Arm...Drove me nuts

An arm that was modeled on the human arm , capable of performing all motions , except those involving the fingers . It used 4 servos and had joints similar to our shoulders and elbow.The arm was made by using cheap Continuous Rotation Servo , which rendered controlling its motion very difficult. Scraped the project in the end , as the amount of time required to program even simple tasks rendered the development of more advanced tasks impractical.

Bluetooth Controlled Robot Car...Unimpressive!

A car made using wood as the base.I used an Arduino to connect the car to Serial Port via Bluetooth.This makes it possible to control the car from a computer. The car is made by using 4 Continuous Servo as the wheel and a Standard Servo for steering. The steering is a bit of as the materials I am using are not sturdy enough. Moreover the servo I use for steer is not powerful enough to overcome the friction between the wheels and the ground. Using a bigger servo would help , but that would cause a dent in my cash reserves.
Future Improvements:
>Make it controllable by an iOS device.iOS uses a different Bluetooth standard and hence new bluetooth dongle has to be brought ($) .
>Substitute the weak servo for a better one for better steering.

3 Bit-Binary-Adder...Slightly Flawed

A binary Adder made up by using OR , AND , XOR gates . The gates are present in the IC chips. This adder is a personal implementation and hence there are flaws present in it . It gives wrong answers when adding 110 and 101. The reason is that the carrying that I am doing during the addition is not proper and leads to errors. I found a schematic which would correct the error but I did not implement it this was my first , slightly complicated, functioning circuit and I did not want to take it apart.
